In the 30th and the 31st centuries, their relationship with the [[United Planets]] fluctuated between uneasy d\u00e9tente and open warfare. When the U.P. first discovered the Khundian civilization, the Khunds responded by attempting to conquer Earth. They nearly succeeded with the assistance of [[Nemesis Kid]], a saboteur whom they planted in the [[Legion of Super-Heroes]]. U.P. worlds which were conquered by the Khundian empire are not spared from this phenomenon, prompting the Khunds to form a temporary alliance with the Legion. Four superpowered Khunds join the Legion: &#39;&#39;&#39;Firefist&#39;&#39;&#39;, a cyborg; his wife &#39;&#39;&#39;Veilmist&#39;&#39;&#39;, a teleporter; &#39;&#39;&#39;Blood Claw&#39;&#39;&#39;, who possesses indestructible claws; and &#39;&#39;&#39;Flederweb&#39;&#39;&#39;, a winged bounty hunter.&lt;ref&gt;&#39;&#39;Legion of Super-Heroes&#39;&#39; (vol. 4) #44 (June 1993)&lt;/ref&gt;\n\nIncluded in the animated undead force that invaded the planet Sklar are corpses of the many deceased members of the Legion.  Blood Claw is killed by Magnetic Kid&#39;s undead body.&lt;ref&gt;&#39;&#39;Legion of Super-Heroes&#39;&#39; (vol. 4) #46 (August 1993)&lt;/ref&gt; Firefist attempts to murder [[Devlin O&#39;Ryan]], but is seemingly killed himself when Devlin&#39;s reflective power repels the Khund&#39;s weapon blast back at him.&lt;ref&gt;&#39;&#39;Legion of Super-Heroes&#39;&#39; (vol. 4) #47 (September 1993)&lt;/ref&gt; The group manages to defeat Mordru, and the Khunds force Veilmist and Flederweb to resign from the Legion.&lt;ref&gt;&#39;&#39;Legion of Super-Heroes&#39;&#39; (vol. 4) #48 (October 1993)&lt;/ref&gt; Some time later, it is revealed that Firefist survived,&lt;ref name = \&quot;LSH57\&quot;&gt;&#39;&#39;Legion of Super-Heroes&#39;&#39; (vol. 4) #57 (May 1994)&lt;/ref&gt; and that the Khunds have initiated a plot to destroy [[Weber&#39;s World]]. Thus, the saga of the Khundian Legionnaires is currently viewed as non-canonical. However, Veilmist, Firefist, and a shot of Flederweb&#39;s arm appeared in &#39;&#39;[[Final Crisis: Legion of 3 Worlds]]&#39;&#39; #5, among dozens of Legionnaires pulled out of alternate realities.&lt;ref&gt;&#39;&#39;Final Crisis: Legion of 3 Worlds&#39;&#39; #5&lt;/ref&gt;\n\n===20th and 21st centuries===\nDuring the 20th and 21st centuries, the Khunds were extreme enemies of the Thanagarians because of their planetary proximity.&lt;ref&gt;&#39;&#39;[[Legends (comics)|Legends]]&#39;&#39;&lt;/ref&gt; They superficially resemble [[human]]s, but average larger and more muscular in build, with pale [[magenta]] skin. Khunds have codified rules of combat. Khund bodies have much denser bone and muscle tissue than humans. The planet Khundia is a heavy gravity world.&lt;ref&gt;As seen in &#39;&#39;Adventure Comics&#39;&#39; #346 (July 1966)&lt;/ref&gt; The Khunds were part of the alien group that attacks Earth in the &#39;&#39;[[Invasion!
