One example is that the film shows the [[Bat-Signal]] working in broad daylight. Another occurs when, despite the fact that the heroes&#39; [[utility belt]]s had been replaced by normal belts with no pockets or pouches for this serial, in order to escape from a vault, Batman pulls the nozzle and hose of an [[Oxy-fuel welding and cutting|oxy-acetylene torch]] from his belt to cut through a steel door (the tanks for the torch are not shown); to compound this mistake, it is a full-sized oxy-acetylene torch that would have been impossible to carry unseen on his person. Harmon and Glut suggest that this was probably scripted to be a miniaturised 3-inch torch, as used in the comics, but the filmmakers improvised in following the directions for a \&quot;blowtorch.\&quot;&lt;ref name=\&quot;GMS\&quot; /&gt;\n\n==Release==\n[[File:Batman&amp;RobinSerialDVDCover.jpg|thumb|DVD cover]]\nIn 1989, [[GoodTimes Entertainment]] released the serial on VHS, splitting the entire serial into two separate, budget-priced tapes recorded in LP mode. The GoodTimes edition was slightly edited, as well, with several minutes of the opening chapter mysteriously cut. In 1997, [[Columbia TriStar Home Video]] re-released the uncut serial (in SP mode) as a complete 2-tape VHS set. [[Sony Pictures Home Entertainment]] released the serial on [[DVD]] in 2005, timed to coincide with the theatrical release of &#39;&#39;[[Batman Begins]]&#39;&#39;. Unlike [[Batman (serial)|its predecessor]], &#39;&#39;Batman and Robin: The Complete 1949 Movie Serial Collection&#39;&#39; has been given a restoration. On February 4, 2014, Mill Creek Entertainment released Gotham City Serials, a two-disc DVD set that includes both the 1943 Batman serial and the 1949 Batman and Robin serial. [[Rifftrax]] released a Video On Demand of the first installment of the short on July 16, 2013, featuring a running mocking commentary from [[Michael J. Nelson]], [[Kevin Murphy (actor)|Kevin Murphy]] and [[Bill Corbett]] of &#39;&#39;[[Mystery Science Theater 3000]]&#39;&#39; fame.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mst3kinfo.com/?p=16136#comments|title=New Short from RiffTrax\u2026 \u00ab Satellite News}}&lt;/ref&gt; The final episode, \&quot;Batman Victorious,\&quot; was released with commentary on September 19, 2014.\n\n==Chapter titles==\n[[Turner Classic Movies]] began airing episodes of &#39;&#39;Batman and Robin&#39;&#39; in June 2015, following one week after the conclusion of airing the previous 1943 serial.
