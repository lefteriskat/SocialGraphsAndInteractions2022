Jones]].\n\n==Publication history==\nBig Science Action debuted in &#39;&#39;Final Crisis Sketchbook&#39;&#39; #1. Some of the founding members of Big Science Action such as Rising Sun, Sunburst, and Goraiko are pre-existing DC Comics characters. Goraiko was originally created by Grant Morrison and Howard Porter in &#39;&#39;JLA&#39;&#39; #26 (February 1999). According to writer Grant Morrison \&quot;these guys were [[Japan|Japan&#39;s]] [[Justice League|JLA]] back in the day, with a ring or halo-shaped base hovering above [[Mount Fuji]]\&quot;.&lt;ref name=\&quot;Final Crisis Sketchbook&#39; 2008\&quot;&gt;As seen in &#39;&#39;Final Crisis Sketchbook&#39;&#39; #1 (May 2008).&lt;/ref&gt;\n\n==History==\nThe Big Science Action team are seen together for the first time as they appeared in the \&quot;Silver Age\&quot; of Japanese superheroics, in a [[Tokyo]] television [[documentary]] [[retrospective]] in the pages of &#39;&#39;Final Crisis&#39;&#39; #2 (June 2008). During this appearance Rising Sun complains about the ongoing process of self [[commodification]] being carried out by modern Japanese superheroes such as the [[Super Young Team]].&lt;ref name=\&quot;Final Crisis Sketchbook&#39; 2008\&quot;/&gt;&lt;ref&gt;&#39;&#39;Final Crisis&#39;&#39; #2 (June 2008)&lt;/ref&gt;\n\nAn updated version of Big Science Action appears in &#39;&#39;Final Crisis Aftermath: Dance&#39;&#39; #4, in this issue Junior Waveman Kimura invites his daughter Shiny Happy Aquazon from the Super Young Team to join the team, and introduces her to new team member Nazo Baluda.&lt;ref name=\&quot;Final Crisis Aftermath 2009\&quot;&gt;&#39;&#39;Final Crisis Aftermath: Dance&#39;&#39; #4 (October 2009)&lt;/ref&gt;\n\nIn the &#39;&#39;[[Watchmen]]&#39;&#39; sequel &#39;&#39;[[Doomsday Clock (comics)|Doomsday Clock]]&#39;&#39;, a variation of Big Science Action is featured called &#39;&#39;&#39;Big Monster Action&#39;&#39;&#39;. Besides Rising Sun, Goriako, and Hammersuit Zero-X, the other members listed are [[Judomaster]], [[Naiad (comics)|Naiad]], Ram, and [[Samurai (Super Friends)|Samurai]].&lt;ref&gt;&#39;&#39;Doomsday Clock&#39;&#39; #6 (July 2018). DC Comics.&lt;/ref&gt;\n\n==Founding members==\n===Boss Bosozuko===\nBoss Bosozuko, is a hotheaded [[motorcycle]] rider. Bosozuko&#39;s hair is made up of nuclear flames as are the tires on his special motorcycle. His name [[B\u014ds\u014dzoku]] translates as \&quot;violent running gang\&quot;, and stems from the Japanese B\u014ds\u014dzoku motorcycle gangs of the 1950s.&lt;ref name=\&quot;Final Crisis Sketchbook&#39; 2008\&quot;/&gt;\n\n===Cosmo Racer===\nThe being known as Cosmo Racer is an [[amnesia]]c extraterrestrial android who requires specials [[crystal]]s to power his \&quot;roller boots\&quot; up for interstellar travel. According to his creator Grant Morrison: \&quot;What only we know is that his \&quot;Maker\&quot; is a monstrous space tyrant who has sent his little herald out to identify and pacify target planets\&quot;. Using his roller boots Cosmo Racer can currently travel at the speed of sound. His body is made from an unknown nearly frictionless alien material similar in texture to terrestrial [[ceramic]]s or [[plastic]]s.&lt;ref name=\&quot;Final Crisis Sketchbook&#39; 2008\&quot;/&gt;\n\n===Goraiko===\n{{see also|International Ultramarine Corps#Goraiko}}\nGoraiko is a psionic construct projected from the mind of an unnamed young Japanese girl who resides in a high tech [[sensory deprivation]] tank. The Goraiko construct only speaks in [[haiku]] and [[mathematical equation]]s. The girl has a [[My Neighbor Totoro|Totoro]] styled doll in the tank that appears to be the inspiration for Goraiko&#39;s shape, but it is unknown if it is necessary for the manifestation of the being. The being known as Goraiko is large and superhumanly strong, two versions of the Goraiko construct are known to exist, the &#39;green version&#39; in Big Science Action and the &#39;gray version&#39; in the Ultramarines. Grant Morrison described about Goraiko: \&quot;I also threw in Goraiko, the \u2018Nuclear Totoro\u2019 character I created for the Ultramarine Corps back in the JLA days and changed his colour as a little tip of the hat to gray Hulk/green Hulk\&quot;. He is a member of the [[International Ultramarine Corps]].&lt;ref name=\&quot;newsarama\&quot;&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.newsarama.com/comics/080721-MorrisonFC02.html|title=Grant Morrison on &#39;&#39;Final Crisis&#39;&#39; #2 &amp;#124; Newsarama.com|publisher=newsarama.com|access-date=2015-09-05}}&lt;/ref&gt;\n\n===Hammersuit Zero-X===\nMiss Kusanagi a Japanese [[student|schoolgirl]], who is also a supersmart science heroine pilots the monster fighting suit of powered armor known as a \&quot;Hammersuit\&quot;. The Hammersuit appears to use [[electric arc|arc]] generating, [[steampunk]] inspired [[Electrolaser|electrically energized]] [[Jackhammer|pneumatic drills]] as weapons. The suit of armor can also act autonomously, and is based on both [[Gundam]] and [[Gigantor]]. An adult and much older Kusanagi appears in &#39;&#39;Final Crisis Aftermath: Dance&#39;&#39; #4, this issue also reveals her name.&lt;ref name=\&quot;Final Crisis Sketchbook&#39; 2008\&quot;/&gt; Despite her much older age, even compared to the now middle-aged Waveman Kimura, she&#39;s still effective, heavily relying on newer and more powerful iterations of the \&quot;Hammersuit\&quot; to \&quot;&#39;&#39;stay competitive&#39;&#39;\&quot;.&lt;ref name=\&quot;ReferenceA\&quot;&gt;&#39;&#39;Final Crisis Aftermath: Dance&#39;&#39; #4 (october 2009)&lt;/ref&gt;\n\n===Junior Waveman===\nRiki Kimura, the Junior Waveman is a member of the Wavemen, an eight-person Japanese version of the [[Sea Devils (comics)|Sea Devils]], formerly led by Senior Waveman Otomo who died while saving Riki Kimura&#39;s life. He was rescued and raised by the Wavemen after his parents were slain by an army of monsters. He is also the father of [[Super Young Team#Shiny Happy Aquazon|Shiny Happy Aquazon]], a member of the Super Young Team. As of &#39;&#39;Final Crisis Aftermath: Dance&#39;&#39; #4, he is now the much older Senior Waveman Kimura.&lt;ref name=\&quot;Final Crisis Sketchbook&#39; 2008\&quot;/&gt;&lt;ref name=\&quot;ReferenceA\&quot;/&gt;\n\n===Rising Sun===\n{{main article|Rising Sun (comics)}}\nIzumi Yasunari, also known as Rising Sun, is a prominent Japanese [[Solar physics|solar physicist]], and a veteran member of the [[Global Guardians]]. A drunken, unshaven and overweight Rising Sun appears in &#39;&#39;Final Crisis Aftermath: Dance&#39;&#39; #1.&lt;ref name=\&quot;ReferenceB\&quot;&gt;&#39;&#39;Final Crisis Aftermath: Dance&#39;&#39; #1 (July 2009)&lt;/ref&gt;\n\n===Sunburst===\nTakeo Sato the original Sunburst was a popular Japanese [[stuntman]] and [[actor]], and a founding member of Big Science Action. He died in &#39;&#39;[[Crisis on Infinite Earths]]&#39;&#39; #12.&lt;ref&gt;&#39;&#39;Crisis on Infinite Earths&#39;&#39; #12 (March 1986)&lt;/ref&gt;\n\n===Ultimon===\nDai Yokohama, the hero known as Ultimon comes from a dynastic line of [[Super Sentai]] dedicated to fighting [[kaiju]], known as the Ultimon Society. The Ultimon Society are a group of Giant Monster Killers who survived the \&quot;Monster Wars\&quot; that nearly destroyed Japan. He and his \&quot;master\&quot; defeated a trio of monsters called the \&quot;Colonizers\&quot;, they were Scarrba the Protector ([[King Ghidorah]]), Kry-Torr the Burrower ([[Baragon]]), and Lorloxx the Layer ([[Gyaos]]). Ultimon&#39;s master died during the battle and he inherited his powers.&lt;ref name=\&quot;Final Crisis Sketchbook&#39; 2008\&quot;/&gt; Recently the ghost of a being calling itself &#39;&#39;&#39;Ultimon Alpha&#39;&#39;&#39; has been appearing in recurring visions witnessed by [[Super Young Team#Most Excellent Superbat|Most Excellent Superbat]], in &#39;&#39;Final Crisis Aftermath: Dance&#39;&#39; #1.&lt;ref name=\&quot;ReferenceB\&quot;/&gt;\n\n==New members==\n===Boss Bishonen===\nBoss Bishonen is a new male member introduced in &#39;&#39;Final Crisis Aftermath: Dance&#39;&#39; #4, he is described by Senior Waveman Kimura as the successor to Boss Bosozuko and \&quot;carries the torch of a grand tradition of fiery champions\&quot;. His name is a reference to [[Bish\u014dnen]], a Japanese term which translates as \&quot;beautiful youth\&quot;.&lt;ref name=\&quot;Final Crisis Aftermath 2009\&quot;/&gt;&lt;ref name=\&quot;i.newsarama.com\&quot;&gt;{{Cite web |url=http://i.newsarama.com/images/FCAD_4-page6-02.jpg |title=Archived copy |access-date=2009-08-17 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110723183806/http://i.newsarama.com/images/FCAD_4-page6-02.jpg |archive-date=2011-07-23 |url-status=dead}}&lt;/ref&gt;\n\n===Nazo Baluda===\nNazo Baluda is a new female member introduced in &#39;&#39;Final Crisis Aftermath: Dance&#39;&#39; #4, she is described by Senior Waveman Kimura as the team&#39;s \&quot;dark star stealth warrior\&quot;. The Japanese word [[wikt:nazo|nazo]] translates as riddle, enigma or mystery. And the name Baluda is a reference to Dominatrix Baluda, a leather clad super villainess from the Japanese [[manga]] and [[anime]] series &#39;&#39;[[Wingman (manga)|Yume No Senshi Uwingu Man]]&#39;&#39; (Dream Warrior Wing Man).&lt;ref name=\&quot;Final Crisis Aftermath 2009\&quot;/&gt;&lt;ref name=\&quot;i.newsarama.com\&quot;/&gt;&lt;ref name=\&quot;japanhero\&quot;&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.japanhero.com/profiles/wingman_profileL.htm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20030605015814/http://japanhero.com/profiles/wingman_profileL.htm|archive-date=2003-06-05|url-status=dead|title=Japan Hero &amp;gt; Super Sentai, Kamen Rider, Tokusatsu Heroes and Anime Heroes|access-date=2015-09-05}}&lt;/ref&gt;\n\n===Shiny Happy Aquazon===\nShiny Happy Aquazon is the daughter of Senior Waveman Kimura the current leader of Big Science Action. She was a founding member of the [[Super Young Team]], and is invited to join Big Science Action by her father in &#39;&#39;Final Crisis Aftermath: Dance&#39;&#39; #4.&lt;ref name=\&quot;Final Crisis Aftermath 2009\&quot;/&gt;&lt;ref name=\&quot;i.newsarama.com\&quot;/&gt; She later declines her membership, with her teammates Atomic Lantern and Superbat reminding her of the obligation she still has with her original group.&lt;ref&gt;&#39;&#39;Final Crisis Aftermath: Dance&#39;&#39; #4&lt;/ref&gt; Waveman Kimura, however, still thinks that her membership in the Big Science Action team is her &#39;&#39;birthright&#39;&#39;.\n\n==Equipment==\nFuji Station is Big Science Action&#39;s halo-shaped base of operations, which hovers above [[Mount Fuji]].
